Why HROC Sits Beside iSN.BiZ

Most companies bolt charitable giving on after they figure out how to make money. iSN.BiZ Inc was structured the other direction: the nonprofit existed first as the reason for the company’s existence, and the for-profit operations exist to serve it.

The thesis is small and load-bearing. There are people the public-benefit system reaches last and supports least — people in active substance use, people exiting incarceration, people without immigration status, people aging out of foster care, people on the wrong side of one bureaucratic decision. They are reachable, but only by organizations willing to operate with low overhead, high trust, and zero moralizing. HROC was incorporated to be one of those organizations.

iSN.BiZ’s automation-first product line — market intelligence, trading-signal infrastructure, OSINT tooling — is engineered to throw off durable revenue with a small operational footprint. That margin is what funds HROC. Every recurring product subscription, every infrastructure contract, every B2B engagement at iSN.BiZ is structured so that meaningful giving to HROC becomes the natural consequence of doing the company well, not a separate charitable initiative we have to remember to fund.

What HROC Actually Does

A narrow operational mission, deliberately. The breadth of need is enormous; the discipline that lets a small nonprofit help anyone at all is refusing to chase scope.

Harm-Reduction Operations

Harm reduction is a public-health stance: meet people where they are, reduce the fatal and disabling consequences of high-risk situations, and let dignity and continuity of contact do the longer work. HROC supports the field-level operational layer that organizations on this stance run on — supplies, distribution logistics, training materials, and the unglamorous infrastructure that determines whether a program reaches anyone at all.

The organization does not duplicate clinical services already provided by hospitals, county programs, or established harm-reduction nonprofits. It supports the gaps — the moments when a partner organization needs a piece of operational backbone that no funder is paying for, but without which an entire program stalls.

Outreach for Communities the System Misses

A second standing area is direct, individual-case outreach for people whose situation has fallen through the structural cracks: a wage claim a worker can’t file alone, an immigration filing tied to a domestic-violence record, a family separation caused by a single bureaucratic timing error. These are not programmatic at scale, but they are catastrophic at the level of one person’s life.

HROC operates these as documented, finite case engagements rather than ongoing case management — identify the leverage point, mobilize the legal or administrative resource, document the outcome, exit. The model is intentionally small, repeatable, and resistant to mission drift.

How the Giving Structure Works

Revenue is the Source

iSN.BiZ Inc operates automation-first revenue products — subscription intelligence, infrastructure tooling, and B2B engagements — engineered for low overhead and durable margin. That margin is the upstream input to all HROC giving.

Giving is Structural

Charitable contribution to HROC is treated as a fixed operating commitment of the for-profit, modeled into the company’s financials at the line-item level. It is not a discretionary end-of-year decision and does not compete with operating expenses for board attention.

HROC Deploys Programmatically

Funds received at HROC are deployed against the documented harm-reduction and outreach mission, with governance records, board oversight, and public reporting structured to remain auditable through the IRS determination process and beyond.

Governance and Current Status

Transparency is the rule, not a deliverable. This is what is currently true about HROC, exactly as true as it is.

Incorporated as a Washington nonprofit corporation

HROC Inc is registered with the Washington Secretary of State and operates under the state’s nonprofit corporation statute. State-level nonprofit standing is established and current.

IRS Form 1023 filed — awaiting federal determination

The application for federal tax-exempt 501(c)(3) recognition has been filed. We are currently in the standard IRS review window. Until the determination letter is issued, we describe HROC accurately as a nonprofit corporation with 501(c)(3) status pending; we do not represent contributions as federally tax-deductible until that letter is in hand.
